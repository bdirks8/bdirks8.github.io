Biography

I am an NSF post-doc/Simons Instructor at Stony Brook University, mentored by Christian Schnell. For the 2024-2025 academic year, I was a Postdoctoral Member at the Institute for Advanced Study. In Spring 2023, I graduated with a PhD in Mathematics from University of Michigan. My advisor was Mircea Mustaţǎ. Before that, I was an undergraduate student at UCLA. I am interested in algebraic geometry. Specifically, I like to think about Hodge modules and their applications to the study of singularities. Some of the topics I have found most interesting are Hodge Ideals, (generalized) V-filtrations and their interaction with the Hodge filtration of Hodge modules, and Bernstein-Sato polynomials. You can find my CV here.

At UCLA, I was a mentor in the LA Math Circle (now the ORMC). I have also acted as a mentor in the Michigan Mathematics DRP.
